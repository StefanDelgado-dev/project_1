Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I start buying a property?

First, identify your budget and preferred location. Then, consult with a licensed real estate broker, view available listings, and schedule site visits. Once you find the perfect property, proceed with reservation and required documentation.

2. What documents do I need?

Typical requirements include:

  • Government-issued ID
  • Proof of income/financial capacity
  • Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  • Reservation agreement
  • Bank statements and proof of funds

3. Are installment plans available?

Yes! Many properties offer flexible installment plans. Terms vary by developer and property type, typically ranging from 5-20 years. Bank financing options are also available for qualified buyers.

4. How do I verify a property is legitimate?

Always verify:

  • Transfer Certificate of Title (TCT) authenticity
  • Developer's credentials and track record
  • Tax declarations and payment history
  • Licensed broker accreditation
  • Property location survey and contracts

5. What are the hidden costs in buying property?

Budget for these additional expenses:

  • Broker's commission (typically 3-5%)
  • Transfer tax and registration fees
  • Notarial fees and documentary stamps
  • Property taxes and utility deposits
  • Home inspection and survey costs

6. Can I get a home loan easily?

Yes, major banks and financing institutions offer competitive mortgage packages. Most require 20-30% downpayment and monthly income at least 3-5 times the monthly amortization. Pre-approval strengthens your negotiating position.

7. What's the best time to buy property?

Property is generally a long-term investment. The best time depends on: your financial readiness, market conditions, available inventory, and personal circumstances. Consult with me to find your ideal timing and property match.

8. What should I know before signing contracts?

Essential due diligence:

  • Read all terms and conditions carefully
  • Understand payment schedule and penalties
  • Verify cancellation and refund policies
  • Confirm warranty and after-sales service
  • Have a lawyer review all documents
